| **[Inversion diameter and 2-edge-colored homomorphisms](https://arxiv.org/abs/2602.24171v1)** | 2026-02-27 | <details><summary>Show</summary><p>In an oriented graph, the inversion of a subset of vertices X is the operation reversing the direction of every arc with both endpoints in X. Given a graph G, the inversion distance between two orientations G is the minimum number of inversions transforming one into the other. The inversion diameter diam(G) is the maximum such distance over all pairs of orientations of G. Through an equivalent formulation of inversions over 2-edge-colorings of G, we introduce the use of homomorphism-universal 2-edge-colored graphs to obtain bounds on the inversion diameter of various classes of graphs. Our first result upper bounds the inversion diameter by a linear function of the acyclic chromatic number, improving on the previous quadratic dependency. We then show that any triangle-free graph G with maximum degree D satisfies diam(G) <= D + log D, making progress on the conjecture of Havet et al. that diam(G) <= D. Finally, we prove a general result about subdivisions: if a graph has inversion diameter k, any of its subdivisions has inversion diameter at most k + log k + 5.</p></details> |  |
